IPL Data Analysis

I conducted an extensive data analysis of the Indian Premier League (IPL) by cleaning and interpreting ball-by-ball data from 2008-2022. Utilizing advanced techniques in pandas and numpy, I performed data cleaning, multi-level aggregation, and visualization using tools such as matplotlib and seaborn. Through my analysis, I uncovered valuable insights that are not readily available on the internet, such as the percentage of batsmen who score at least 30 runs in IPL and the percentage of bowlers who pick up a wicket in each match, among others. I also created and analyzed granular performance data in different stages of the match, including powerplay, middle-overs, death, first innings, and second innings, for metrics such as strike rate (SR), average (AVG), economy rate (ECO), not outs, wickets, and runs. To effectively communicate my findings and gain a deeper understanding of performance trends in batting and bowling, I created comprehensive visualizations. Additionally, I conducted an in-depth analysis of players who were part of the IPL Auction 2023, extracting valuable insights from the data and shared the results on the r/Cricket subreddit, where it received positive feedback from redditors
